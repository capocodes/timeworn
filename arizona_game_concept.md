**Scope:** This file is the **creative and tonal north star** (setting, teaser, visual identity, factions as flavor). **Product and session shape** (extraction loop, tiers/trains, slice, multiplayer) are defined in **`GAME_DESIGN_DOCUMENT.md`**, **`01_vision/game_loop.md`**, and **`01_vision/core_extraction_loop.md`**. If anything here conflicts with those, **update the GDD and vision docs first**, then revise this summary.

## Core Premise
**Timeworn** is a cinematic, atmospheric game set in **Arizona (circa 1858–1862)** that blends:
- Western frontier history
- Apache / Union conflict themes
- Sci-fi intrusion (massive ancient or unknown mech/robot presence)

The tone is mysterious, grounded, and slowly revealing something *deeply unnatural*.

---

## High-Level Concept
Two opposing forces:
- **Union soldiers**
- **Apache raiders**

…are both drawn into the same conflict.

They are:
- Initially hostile to each other
- Forced into a shared moment of awe and fear

Because of:
> A massive, deadly, unexplained mechanical entity

---

## Narrative Tone & Style
- Slow, cinematic reveals
- High contrast visuals (silhouettes, dusk/dawn lighting)
- Mystery-first storytelling
- Minimal exposition — show, don’t tell
- “Something is wrong” feeling before explanation

Inspirations (implied from direction):
- Western films
- Sci-fi intrusion (War of the Worlds / Horizon vibes)
- Unreal Engine cinematic realism

## Weapons Concept (Early Direction)

Two example weapons:
1. **Modified Springfield Model 1861**
   - Still single-action
   - Enhanced reload speed via advanced modifications
   - Blends historical + futuristic tech

2. **Sawn-off Coach Gun**
   - Dual 12-gauge blast capability
   - Close-range power weapon

### Design Philosophy
- Historical authenticity base
- Sci-fi augmentation layered on top
- “Timeworn but advanced”
